Let's explore data on romance scams and build a model to track down the scammers and
improve the safety of our online dating community.
Online dating has been growing in popularity. However, people have reported losing over
$1.3 billion related to romance scams in the past five years, with the most significant
reported losses paid in cryptocurrency (Fletcher, 2022). By exploring the scams data and the
monetary loss will help us gain insight into the trend of dating scams and build innovative
features to protect the users.
To begin, we will use the published fraud report to the Federal Trade Commission (FTC)
Consumer Sentinel Network classified as romance scams in the context of age group, social
media platforms, and payment methods indicating the dollar losses. Then, we can virtually
explore the intersection trend of the data and understand the target audience for the
scammers, the top online platforms identified as the starting point for the scam, the top
payment methods on romance scams ranked by reported monetary losses. Furthermore, we
can use Rstudio to build a regression model to virtualize the correlation between romance
scam trend analysis and Tinder's database to observe the users' behavior patterns and track
down the scammers.
The project will use cutting-edge technology to help accomplish a minimum for user safety,
which aligns with the company goal as Elie Seidman (2020), CEO of Tinder, mentioned that
"driving our safety work forward at an unmatched scale".
